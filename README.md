How to Use the Dashboard

The Month Selector
The month selector is in the top-right corner of the app bar. It is the primary control for the entire dashboard.

ALL (default) — shows yearly totals and averages across all 12 months of 2025. Charts display the full year at once.
Any specific month (e.g., "March") — filters every card and chart to that month only. Summary cards update to show that month's numbers, and charts highlight the selected period.


Summary Cards
The four cards at the top give you a quick snapshot:

CardWhat it showsTotal ShipmentsVolume of freight shipments processedOn-Time RatePercentage of shipments delivered on timeOpen ExceptionsCount of unresolved shipment issuesBest RegionThe top-performing region for the selected period

Each card also shows a ▲ or ▼ indicator in green or red, reflecting whether the metric improved or declined compared to the previous month.

Charts
Shipment Volume (bar chart, left) — shows how many shipments were processed each month. Look for seasonal peaks.
On-Time Delivery Rate (line chart, right) — tracks delivery reliability over time. Compare this side-by-side with the bar chart to spot patterns.
Open Exceptions (area chart, full width) — shows when exception volumes spike. These often correspond with high-shipment months.


Regional Breakdown
The table at the bottom breaks down shipments and on-time rates by region (North, South, East, West) for the selected period.


Key Question This Dashboard Answers
Does higher shipment volume hurt on-time delivery performance?

Use the month selector to step through months with notably high shipment volumes (typically summer months) and compare the On-Time Rate card and chart. If the line dips when the bars are tallest, that's your correlation — and a conversation starter for the ops team.
